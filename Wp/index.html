About Our Initiative

We are a group of passionate students in Bangalore who couldn't ignore the suffering of stray cats in our city. Every day, countless felines die from hunger, disease, or predator attacks, and many existing organizations fail to address this crisis adequately.

What started as a small effort to feed a few cats in our neighborhood has grown into a dedicated rescue operation. We provide food, medical care, and safe shelters for stray cats, focusing on areas where they are most vulnerable.

Unlike some organizations that misuse funds, we guarantee that 100% of donations go directly to helping the cats. We document and share all our activities transparently so you can see the impact of your support.

Our Mission & Values

Why do we donate or support you, as there are many other organizations and camps who do it much better, with wider operations?

We understand this question completely - it's natural to wonder why you should support a small student group when larger organizations exist. Here's why we're different:

We've personally documented cases where some organizations sell rescued cats or misuse donation funds. While we won't name them yet (until we're more established), we promise absolute transparency in how we use every rupee.

As young students, we have no ulterior motives - just a pure desire to help these vulnerable creatures. We don't take salaries or expenses from donations. Every contribution goes directly to food, medicine, and shelter materials.

We focus on areas others ignore, rescuing cats that would otherwise die unnoticed. Even a single rupee can save a life when used responsibly. Join us in making a real difference where it's needed most.

What exactly do we do?
  • We try for a daily feeding programs in neglected areas of Bangalore
  • Emergency medical care for injured/sick cats
  • Building and maintaining safe shelters
  • TNR (Trap-Neuter-Return) programs to control population humanely
  • Rescue operations for cats in immediate danger
  • Education programs about stray cat welfare
